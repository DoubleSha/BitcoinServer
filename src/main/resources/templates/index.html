    FAQ
  • Why is DoubleSha needed?

    Sending a payment Bitcoin today is painful. The process of copying and pasting a long, inscrutable address into your wallet software and irreversibly sending money away is scary. Was it 0.002 BTC or 0.02 BTC? Did you remember to include miner fees? Are you sure that address you copied actually belongs to the person you think it does?

    Using Bitcoin links instead of directly copying and pasting Bitcoin addresses is more secure and much easier for everyone involved. We believe it is the future of Bitcoin.

  • What is a bitcoin link?

    A Bitcoin link is a special link that, when clicked, opens an external Bitcoin wallet application that is installed on the user's computer. Think of it like a shortened url for Bitcoin payments. No more dealing with Bitcoin addresses.

    When a user clicks on a bitcoin link, their wallet is opened, and the payment information you entered is displayed for the user to either accept or cancel. Once they accept, their wallet automatically creates a payment directly to you.

  • What wallets are supported?

    Today, you can use Bitcoin links with the latest version of Bitcoin-QT (Bitcoin Core) or on mobile with the Android Bitcoin Wallet.

    Support for Bitcoin links in more wallets is coming soon, including Multibit, Hive, and Mycelium.

  • How do Bitcoin links work?

    Bitcoin links are a part of the Payment Protocol, a new development by the Bitcoin open source developers. The Payment Protocol is an industry standard for Bitcoin wallets to communicate without the need to expose Bitcoin addresses directly to users. Similarly to how most users use the internet today without knowing what an IP address is, with the Payment Protocol, most users will be able to use Bitcoin without knowing what a Bitcoin address is.

    If you want the gory technical detail, this video gives a great overview of the technical details of the Payment Protocol.

  • How does DoubleSha protect my privacy?

    DoubleSha is completely trustless, meaning we do not know your private keys or any information about you. Using DoubleSha is no less private than traditional Bitcoin payments, and it is not possible for us to lose any of your hard-earned Bitcoin if our servers are compromised.
